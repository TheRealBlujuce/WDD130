Home

The Home Page will simple. It will sort of have an about me section describing what I do with the website and the kind of information they will be presented with. I will also add on personal experience and provide information on how games are rated. There will not be images on the home screen, since the home screen is just for navigation seeing how this is a blog website. Because of this, all the real content will be in actual posts themselves. Because of this, navigation is tied to posts, and not to a navigation bar. Rating will be determined using a star system where each star represents an age group. 1 star = 10+, 2 stars = 15+, and 3 stars = 17+. Each game reviewed will have specific sections of review: Gameplay, Graphics, Music, and Potential Benefits. Gameplay will be about what you as the player or in this case, your child, will be doing in the game. What is there to do? Is it too violent for a younger child? Are there things to do besides something violent? Graphics will describe what the art-style is like, as well as special effects. Is there blood shows? if so, how much blood is shown? Is it pure gore, or is it not significant enough to desensitise the child? Are the graphics cartoony or hyper-realistic? The Music section will describe the music of the game. Some parents only let their children listen to certain types of music. So is the music more calming and relaxing? Is it invigorating? Does it entice energy or relaxation based on the gameplay? And finally, the Potential Benefits section will describe what it actually provides, skill-wise to a child (or adult even!). Does it promote problem-solving? does it require that you work in a group? Do you need to prepare to meet certain expectations? Does it help with your hand-eye coordination? Many people believe that video games make children out to be criminals, overly violent, and bad adults. However, it has been proven that this is not the case. This section will also point out certain behaviors and emotions children may have while playing the game, and tips on how to help the child process their emotions in a manner that will help them find joy in game, and out.
